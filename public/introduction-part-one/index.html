Sep 16th, 2012

Front Matter | About

’m standing here in the printing room at the Plantin-Moretus Museum in Antwerp Belgium. This was the stately town-home, and the printing and publishing house of Christophe Plantin, a famous 16th Century publisher.[2]

In over 400 years, very little has changed in this house, in fact, the year could very well be 1582.

It’s as though Christophe, his assistant Jan Moretus, and their staff of type founders, compositors, printers and proofreaders could walk in at any moment and begin their day’s work.

The machinery and tools are all here and still in good working order.


  • Tools of the Trade.

    Photograph by Museum Plantin-Moretus 3

The end result of this labour intensive process of typesetting, composition, printing, proofreading, binding and publishing is, the mass-produced book.

And this museum contains thousands of valuable antique books on display behind glass and wire.
