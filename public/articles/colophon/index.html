The Book of Jack began on a piece of loose leaf paper and was initially conceived to be a ‘Coffee Table’ book, filled with imagery and supporting text.

The initial research consisted of Internet searches at a time (back in 1994) when the Web was still in its infancy. This was 6 years before Google came to prominence and almost ten years before Wikipedia contained more than 250,000 English articles.

Connection was through a dedicated telephone line with a 56K dial-up modem, providing speeds no faster than 56 kbits/sec.

At this time, there were about 10 million people online reading through about 10,000 websites worldwide! 3


  • The Book of Jack begins on paper.

    Illustration by Mark Schipper 4

The Internet and Web have revolutionized how information is shared.

The Book of Jack would not be possible without the generosity of a large number of people.

Today, bookofjack.org is powered by Octopress and hosted by Heroku.5

As a framework, Octopress already has a lot of functionality out-of-the-box, so I didn't have to build everything from scratch, but I have added a number of customizations.


  • Octopress Logo.

    Illustration from octopress.org 4

Jouni Lintu's Notify plugin is used to automatically reload these pages as I am writing them, saving me a few keystrokes and some time.

Balazs Kutil's Twitter Bootstrap theme is used for layout and styling.

Dan Watson's Category List plugin is used to create the 'Table of Contents'.

I created a tag list plugin to create the 'Related Articles' feature.

Wong Liang Zan's Popular Posts plugin is used to generate the 'Popular Articles'.
